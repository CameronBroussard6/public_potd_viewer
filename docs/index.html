I will also be sprinkling a Grisham homer today --- **Stat Key** *(custom stats I built - basis of a lot of my decision making, silly names but easy to distinguish):* **CLAW+:** Context-weighted offensive impact per plate appearance, standardized so 100 = league average and +20 = 1 standard deviation above. The goal was to measure how impactful a hitter is *context dependent*. A solo homer in the 2nd inning of a 7-0 game doesn't get as much love as a solo homer in the 8th of a 2-2 game. Moving runners over, not striking out with RISP, grinding out long ABs, hitting with RISP, and doing damage with runners on all contribute. Think of it as a "frustration" index. A 140 CLAW+ hitter probably isn't frustrating to watch because he always seems to do his job. **FANG+:** The pitching mirror of CLAW+, same 100-centered scale, standardized within role (SP vs RP) since they face different leverage. Strikeouts earn the biggest bonus in RISP spots, GIDPs add credit, and hits/walks allowed are penalized by the actual damage they caused.
