This means a lot when he's facing Keegan Smith, a low-Challenger player with three career grass matches (all this year). Last week in Birmingham, he got back on track by dispatching ATP #78 Mattia Bellucci with ease before a reasonable loss to Arthur Fery in 3 sets. Bolt is first-and-foremost a serve-and-forehand player, but that doesn't mean he's easy to prep for. Most players have trouble handling his lefty slider on slick, fresh grass, and he has enough pop on the forehand to take advantage of weak returns. Alex's serve will be affected as well, but it might be easier for him to deal with than for a taller server like Smith who has a higher ball toss and relies a lot on hitting small targets with pace. If this turns into an unlikely grindfest, Bolt's experience should be enough to push him through, and his slice may prove to be a useful tool.
